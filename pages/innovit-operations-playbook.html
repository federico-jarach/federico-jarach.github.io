INNOVIT Operations Playbook

2023–2024 — Archived (left role)

What It Is

A comprehensive no-code operations system built in Notion that became the single source of truth for INNOVIT — a public-private accelerator helping Italian startups enter the US market. The playbook managed programs, mentors, founders, sessions, alumni, reporting, and cross-continental coordination between San Francisco and Italy.

The Challenge

When I joined as Program Manager in early 2023, the organization had just launched its first programs. We were a small team working across two continents (9 time zones apart), with multiple stakeholders — investors, government officials, partners — all needing real-time data. Information was scattered across Google Sheets, PDFs, Slack messages, and emails. No centralized system. No templates. No way to run multiple programs in parallel.

We needed operational systems that could grow with us — without growing headcount at the same rate.

My Approach

Started small — pitched the concept via a single Notion page mimicking an email, showing leadership what was possible. Got the green light and built incrementally:

  • Program guides — replaced scattered PDFs with a single living document per cohort
  • Centralized databases — templates, sessions, mentors, all linked and queryable
  • Automated workflows — tracking mentor participation, monitoring session progress, generating reports
  • Cross-continental collaboration — async-first design so teams in Italy and SF could work without waiting for each other
  • Notion
  • Notion Automations
  • Google Workspace
  • Slack
  • Loom

Results

  • 150% increase in cohorts — from 4 to 10 cohorts/year without expanding the team
  • 370+ sessions organized across 7 cohorts I directly managed
  • 320-member alumni community built from scratch
  • 700+ mentor/investor network activated across SF and Europe
  • Parallelized planning — multiple programs running simultaneously instead of sequentially
  • Real-time reporting — what used to take hours became a few clicks

What I Learned

No system is ever complete — only evolving. The key was gathering feedback continuously and adapting the workspace to how people actually worked, while preserving core functionality. Also: pitching internal tools requires the same care as pitching external products. You're selling a change in behavior.

What's Private

The actual Notion workspace, templates, and internal documentation are proprietary to INNOVIT. What I've shared here is the methodology, approach, and results — the playbook itself remains with the organization.
