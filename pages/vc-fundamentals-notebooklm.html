VC Fundamentals — NotebookLM

January 2025 — Live

What It Is

An interactive knowledge base built on Google's NotebookLM, trained on the full curriculum from VC Lab's Venture Institute. It's a way to query, explore, and connect concepts from the program materials — like having a study partner that's read everything and remembers it all.

Why I Built It

I got accepted to VC Lab's Venture Institute to fill a gap in my experience: I've worked with startups, accelerators, and founders — but never from the investor side. The materials are dense. I wanted a faster way to learn, review, and make connections across topics. NotebookLM let me build that.

My Process

Uploaded the Venture Institute sprint materials (PDFs, docs) into NotebookLM. Structured the sources by topic. Then started querying — testing how well it could synthesize across documents, surface relevant concepts, and answer questions I'd actually ask.

  • NotebookLM
  • VC Lab Venture Institute

What I Learned

NotebookLM is surprisingly good at connecting ideas across sources — better than I expected for a "chat with your docs" tool. The limitation is depth: it's great for exploration and review, less so for nuanced analysis. Best used as a complement to actually reading the material, not a replacement.

On the VC side: fund mechanics, LP/GP dynamics, portfolio construction, and how VCs actually think about risk. Still learning.
