LinkedIn Signal System

2025–2026 — Active

What It Is

A personal content operating system I call Personal Platform Growth. It uses LLMs as a thinking partner to research, draft, critique, and iterate LinkedIn posts and carousels, while keeping my voice and the SF-Europe translation sharp.

The Challenge

Personal-brand content breaks for the same reasons ops break: unclear inputs, inconsistent quality, too much reinventing the wheel, and drafting that expands to fill infinite time. I wanted a workflow that let me publish consistently without sounding like a generic AI influencer, while keeping high standards for clarity, honesty, and structure.

My Process

I built a pipeline and a set of reusable content agents, then iterated based on real outputs and feedback.

  • Signal capture to framing — convert raw inputs into a clear thesis, audience, and why-now.
  • Truth and nuance guardrails — force uncertainty labeling, source checks, and ban vague hype language.
  • Voice system — explicit constraints to keep the tone operator-to-operator, not technical or cocky.
  • Draft to critique to rewrite — separate outlining, drafting, self-critique, and tightening so quality is systematic.
  • Repurposing engine — turn one idea into a post, a carousel, a first comment, and short replies without duplicating work.
  • Workflow automation — CLI orchestration to reuse templates and keep outputs versioned.

Tooling

I use ChatGPT with custom project instructions, plus Claude Code and Codex in the CLI. When a claim is time-sensitive, I do quick web verification before I ship.

Results

  • Faster publishing — shorter time from idea to publish-ready draft.
  • Less blank page time — reusable outlines and critique loops keep me moving.
  • Validation effort cut by 90–100% — I offload source checks and signal verification to agentic LLMs using Claude for Chrome.
  • Clearer writing — fewer technical assumptions, stronger framing, and better narrative momentum.
  • Extensible system — ready to expand into a research-to-writing harness for Substack.

What I Learned

The hard part is staying consistent without losing my human voice. The key is constraints that keep the text specific, honest, and grounded. Splitting the workflow into roles (researcher, editor, skeptic, stylist) is more reliable than one mega-prompt.

I also found that one post per terminal window or chat beats one huge context window. When the context gets too big, the hallucination risk goes up.

What's Private

The exact prompt library, scoring rubrics, and workflow harness are my proprietary toolkit. I can share the architecture and anonymized before-and-after drafts, but not the full instruction set.
